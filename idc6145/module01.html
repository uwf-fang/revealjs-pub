What is Big Data

  • Definition

    Big Data is a term that refers to datasets that are so large and complex that traditional data processing tools and methods are no longer sufficient to handle them. In other words, Big Data is characterized by its size, complexity, and speed of creation.

  • Characteristics: Value, Volume, Velocity, Variety, Veracity

  • Big data in various domains

  • Big data compared to traditional data

Application Domains

  • Social media: Facebook, Twitter, etc.

  • Content provider: Netflix, Youtube, etc.

  • Health care: IBM Watson Health, CDC, etc.

  • Finance: Goldman Sachs, American Express, etc.

  • Retail: Amazon, Walmart, etc.

  • More domains: ChatBot, Internet of things (IOT), Sensor network, etc.
