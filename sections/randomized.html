Randomized Algorithms

Xingang (Ian) Fang

Overview

  • A randomized algorithm is an algorithm that employs a degree of randomness as part of its logic.

  • A special paradigm that focus on techniques used in the design of algorithms

  • Combined with other paradigms to produce efficient algorithms

  • Not the leading paradigm in algorithm design

  • Characteristics

    • nondeterministic

    • probabilistic

Core Concepts

Pseudo-random number generator (PRNG)

  • A deterministic algorithm that simulates randomness

  • Use a random seed to generate a sequence of real random numbers

  • Designed to simulate various distributions, e.g. uniform, normal, etc.

    • We mostly use uniform distribution in this course

    • Uniform distribution: equal probability of all outcomes

Random sampling

  • Select a random sample from a set of items

  • With or without replacement (allow or not allow repetition of items)

Randomized Algorithms

  • Genetic algorithms

    • Random initial population

    • Tournament selection/roulette wheel selection

    • Random mutation

    • Random crossover

    • Random replacement

  • Monte Carlo algorithms

    • Approximate solutions to hard problems

    • Employs statistics of random samples to estimate metrics of interest like probability, mean, variance, integral, etc.

    • Examples

      • Approximate the value of pi

      • Approximate the value of an integral

  • Las Vegas algorithms

    • Always produce correct results

    • Randomness is used to improve efficiency

    • Examples

      • Quicksort with random pivot selection

Monte Carlo: Estimation of Pi

  • Problem: Estimate the value of pi

  • Solution: Randomly sample points in a square and count the number of points that fall in a circle

  • The ratio of the number of points in the circle to the total number of points is the ration of the area of the circle to the area of the square

  • Thus, \(\pi = 4 \times ratio\)
